Pro Tips for Success

Concept & Pre-Production

Research real products. Look at how luxury brands (Dior, Chanel, Tom Ford) photograph their beauty products. Use these as moodboards.
Simple products work best. Choose a perfume bottle, cream jar, or lipstick—not complex items. Shape and materials matter more than complexity.
Story first. Before you model, know your story: Is this a luxury product? A natural/organic brand? An edgy, modern aesthetic? Your narrative drives every decision.
Create a storyboard. Even rough sketches help you plan camera angles, lighting mood, and animation timing before you start modeling.

Modeling & Texturing

Clean topology matters. Good edge flow = better deformation and shading. Don't be lazy with your models.
Reference images are your friend. Use high-res photos of the actual product at multiple angles. Model accurately, not generically.
Material accuracy = realism. Glass, plastic, metal—each reflects light differently. Spend time on Fresnel, roughness, and IOR (Index of Refraction) values.
Use proper naming conventions. Name your layers/objects logically (product_cap, product_bottle, etc.). It saves time and looks professional.

Lighting & Rendering

Three-point lighting is your foundation. Key light (main), fill light (soften shadows), back light (separation). Then add accent lights for product details.
Use HDRI for photorealism. Instead of flat lights, use High Dynamic Range Images for natural, realistic reflections on shiny surfaces.
Render in passes. Separate beauty, shadow, AO, emission, etc. This lets your compositor fine-tune colors without re-rendering.
Don't over-render. Higher samples = longer render times. Find the balance: 128-256 samples usually looks great without eating time.

Animation & Commercial

Slow, deliberate movements work best. Shake the camera slowly, rotate the product smoothly. Avoid twitchy, fast cuts—they feel amateur.
Use easing curves. Straight linear animation looks robotic. Add easing in/out to make movement feel organic.
Focus on your product. The camera should reveal the product's best features. Angle it to show shape, material quality, and texture.
Sound design matters. A subtle ambient track or product sound (spray, cap opening) adds polish. Silence or bad audio kills the vibe.
Keep it to 15 seconds. Tell your story fast. Hook viewers in the first 3 seconds, showcase the product in the middle, end with a clear message.

File Management & Submission

Save incrementally NOW. Use Ctrl+Alt+S in Blender every major step. You need 10 saves as proof of workflow. Start early.
Organize your project folder. Separate textures, models, renders, video, references. Clean files = easier debugging.
Export test footage early. Don't wait until the last day to render. Test your video export settings now.
Proof-read your PDF report. Grammar, spelling, and clear explanations matter. The rubric is half your grade.
